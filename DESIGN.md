# Design System

## 1. Overview
This is an online memorial page, likely generated by a templated platform. The design is somber, elegant, and focused on readability to honor the deceased. The use of serif fonts and a muted color palette contributes to a respectful tone. The platform is not explicitly identified by CSS variables.

## 2. Colors
- **Primary Text:** `rgb(104, 85, 76)` (a warm, dark brown)
- **Secondary Text/Accent:** `rgb(28, 152, 160)` (a muted teal, used for links and button borders)
- **Background:** `rgb(241, 231, 221)` (a light, warm beige)
- **Card/Content Background:** `rgb(255, 255, 255)` (white, with a subtle transparency for overlays)
- **Border/Divider:** `rgb(193, 169, 141)` (a light brown, used for subtle dividers and borders)
- **Error/Highlight:** `rgb(128, 34, 34)` (a dark red, defined as `--color--error` but not visually prominent in the screenshot)

**Brand Accent Color:** The muted teal (`rgb(28, 152, 160)`) used for the "Remember Me" button border and other interactive elements is the accent. It's subtle but provides visual cues for action.

## 3. Typography
- **Font Families:**
    - **Serif (Headings & Body):** `Georgia, Times, serif` (implied by the visual style and `sloop-script-three` which is decorative, combined with a `copperplate` like display font for names)
    - **Sans-serif (Subheadings & Body):** `Inter, Helvetica, Arial, Verdana, sans-serif`
- **Headings (Main Name):** A large decorative serif font, visually similar to `sloop-script-three, sans-serif` for "In Loving Memory" and `copperplate, serif` for the name itself.
    - "In Loving Memory": `39.52px` to `63.52px`
    - Name: `63.52px`, `400` weight
- **Subheadings (Section Titles):** `Inter` or similar sans-serif, `26px` to `31.52px`, `700` weight, all caps, letter-spacing `0.5px`.
- **Body Text:** `Inter` or similar sans-serif, `16px`, `400` weight, `24px` line-height.
- **Micro Text:** `12px`, `18px` line-height (used for date/location details).

## 4. Spacing & Layout
- **Container Width:** Centered narrow content, with a max-width that keeps text columns readable. The main content area has significant horizontal `padding` (e.g., `0px 224px`).
- **Vertical Stacking:** The page is structured as a series of vertically stacked full-width sections.
- **Section Spacing:** Generous vertical `padding` between sections (e.g., `60px 0px`).
- **Internal Spacing:**
    - `20px` for general paragraph and element separation.
    - `8px` for smaller vertical spacing.
    - `40px` for larger horizontal padding around content blocks (e.g., within card grids).

## 5. Photography & Visual Style
The site uses real photography, primarily for the featured memorial image.
- **Featured Image:** A contained, prominent portrait of the deceased, framed with a border. This image is the primary emotional carrier, presented candidly and warmly.
- **Tributes & Memories Images:** Smaller, contained images within a grid-like layout, accompanying personal stories. These appear to be candid or personal photographs, reinforcing the authentic, personal nature of the memorial.

## 6. Elevation & Shadows
- **Subtle Box Shadows:**
    - `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.25) 0px 2px 8px 0px` (darker, for the main memorial image frame)
    - `rgb(236, 236, 236) 0px 1px 3px 0px` (lighter, used for cards or subtle UI elements)

## 7. Border & Shape
- **Border Radius:**
    - `4px` (subtle rounding on interactive elements like buttons and input fields).
    - `6px` (slightly more rounded corners, potentially on image frames).
    - `100px` or `100%` for circular elements (e.g., the share button).
- **Borders:**
    - `rgb(193, 169, 141)` (light brown, `1px` solid, used for dividers and subtle framing).
    - `rgb(28, 152, 160)` (teal, `1px` solid, used for accent on buttons).

## 8. Components
- **Primary Button:** "Remember Me" button with a teal `rgb(28, 152, 160)` border, `4px` border-radius, and sans-serif uppercase text.
- **Secondary Button/Link:** "Create a Memorial" appears as a simple text link with a `1px` underline.
- **Share Icon:** A circular icon with a subtle shadow and light grey border, containing a sharing symbol.
- **Info Card:** Used for event details, with a white background, subtle border, and internal padding.
- **Tribute Card:** A two-column layout within "TRIBUTES & MEMORIES" section, featuring an image on one side and text on the other. Each card has a white background and subtle shadow.

## 9. Content Structure Pattern
The dominant content structure pattern is **vertically stacked full-width sections**, each with a clear heading. Within these sections, there are sub-patterns:
- **Centered Narrow Content:** The "REMEMBERING MARGARET" section and the main memorial details are presented in a single, narrow, centered column for focused reading.
- **Two-Column Grid (Tributes):** The "TRIBUTES & MEMORIES" section uses a two-column layout to display individual stories and accompanying images. This creates a more dynamic, yet still organized, presentation of personal anecdotes.
- **Event Listing:** The "CELEBRATING MARGARET'S LIFE" section utilizes a clear, tabular-like structure for event details.

## 10. Brand Voice
The brand voice is **reverent, gentle, and commemorative**. It focuses on celebrating a life lived, using warm, inviting language. The copy emphasizes memory, honor, and connection. Examples include:
- "In Loving Memory"
- "Remember, celebrate, and honor a life well lived."
- "Remembering Margaret"
- "Celebrating Margaret's Life"
- "Tributes & Memories"
- "Share a Memory"
The language is formal yet personal, appropriate for a memorial context, avoiding overly casual or overly somber tones.

## 11. Brand Assets & Key Concepts
- **Named concepts:** "A Remembered Life" (from the site title).
- **Organizations:** Mentions "American Red Cross" and "Wounded Warrior Project" as recipients for donations, indicating values of service and compassion.

## 12. Do's and Don'ts
**Do's:**
- Maintain a respectful and warm tone in all content.
- Use serif fonts for headings and body text to convey elegance and tradition.
- Prioritize readability with ample line-height and narrow content columns.
- Incorporate personal, candid photography to enhance emotional connection.
- Utilize the muted teal accent color subtly for calls to action and important links.
- Emphasize vertical stacking of sections for clear content hierarchy.

**Don'ts:**
- Avoid bright, clashing, or overly saturated colors.
- Do not use overly informal language or slang.
- Refrain from dense text blocks; break content into manageable paragraphs.
- Do not use generic stock photography that lacks personal connection to the deceased.
- Avoid large, aggressive typography that might distract from the memorial tone.
- Do not use complex, multi-column layouts that could overwhelm the user.
